About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Law, Science and Technology at the University of Bologna and in Artificial Intelligence at Universidad Politécnica de Madrid. My research focuses on the intersection of law, technology, and artificial intelligence, with a particular interest in democratic deliberation, digital rights, and ethical AI.

With a background in Computer Engineering and experience in data analysis, I combine technical expertise with a deep understanding of the social and legal implications of technology. I am passionate about using technology to address social challenges and promote digital rights.

I'm involved in various initiatives related to technopolitics, digital rights, and mutualism. In my free time, I enjoy Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, soccer, reading, music, and hiking.
